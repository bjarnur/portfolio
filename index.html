King of the Cube

The King of the Cube is a multiplayer, augmented reality game inspired by classic games such as Donkey Kong. The project started as a group project in the course Adcanved Graphics and Interaction at KTH. Together with five other students, I worked on the game from August till December 2018, some of us are still working on the project today, and we have plans to make it available to the public this year. I have been involved with most aspects of making the game, such as integrating the game with AR technologies and working on gameplay featues. My main responsibilities have been working on networking features, so the game can support multiple players, and working on custom graphics, such as smoke an explosions.

A Tonne of Trees

A project in the course Advanced Topics in Computer Graphics, the aim of the projecrt was to reproduce some of the methods presented in the paper Real-time Realistic Rendering and Lighting of Forests by Bruneton and Neyret. The project was a great opportunity for me to get familiar with modern OpenGL, and throughout the project work I got a lot of help from learnopengl.com.

Information Visualization for Inno Energy

Worked on a information visualization project for InnoEnergy along with eight other students. The project was part of the course Information Visualization at KTH. On this project I worked mostly on the back end and the processing of data. I was also the main contact with the InnoEnergy, and I discussed the project requirements and progress with them throughout the course of the project. The project is hosted on http://bjarnig.com/.
